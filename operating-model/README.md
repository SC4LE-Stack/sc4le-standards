# SC4LE Operating Model

The SC4LE Operating Model defines how federated enablement, governance, and continuous improvement work together to create a scalable, adaptive, and ethically grounded organisation.  
It provides the structural blueprint for how shared principles, Local Design Authorities (LDAs), the Central Design Authority (CDA), and operational teams interact to deliver value with clarity, autonomy, and alignment.

The model is intentionally lightweight, principle‑led, and designed to reduce cognitive load while enabling high‑quality decision‑making across complex environments.

---

# 1. Purpose of the Operating Model

The Operating Model exists to:

- Provide a **federated governance structure** that balances autonomy with alignment.  
- Enable **local decision‑making** within clear strategic boundaries.  
- Embed **shared principles** consistently across domains.  
- Support **continuous sensing and improvement** through AI‑enabled feedback loops.  
- Create a repeatable, scalable mechanism for establishing and maturing LDAs.  
- Ensure that transformation is **adaptive, evidence‑based, and ethically grounded**.  

The Operating Model is not a process.  
It is the **infrastructure** that enables consistent, high‑quality decisions and flow across the organisation.

---

# 2. Core Components of the Operating Model

The SC4LE Operating Model is built around five structural elements:

## 2.1 Shared Principles (Centre)
Shared Principles form the behavioural and decision‑making core of SC4LE.  
They provide alignment without bureaucracy and act as the “source code” for all governance, design, and delivery decisions.

Referenced file:  
- `/foundations/shared-principles.md`

---

## 2.2 Central Design Authority (CDA)
The CDA is the enduring hub of federated governance.  
It ensures coherence across domains by:

- Interpreting and stewarding shared principles  
- Managing cross‑domain decisions  
- Maintaining the SC4LE Standard  
- Providing architectural and ethical guardrails  
- Supporting LDAs with guidance and escalation pathways  

The CDA does **not** centralise control — it maintains coherence.

---

## 2.3 Local Design Authorities (LDAs)
LDAs provide contextual governance within domains.  
They:

- Interpret shared principles locally  
- Own domain‑specific decisions  
- Ensure governance fit for local context  
- Support operational teams  
- Escalate only when thresholds require it  

LDAs are the mechanism that enables **federated autonomy**.

---

## 2.4 Operational Teams
Operational teams deliver value and make the majority of decisions.  
They:

- Apply shared principles  
- Make reversible, low‑risk decisions locally  
- Surface signals through sensing loops  
- Collaborate with LDAs for contextual guidance  

Teams are the **primary decision‑makers** in SC4LE.

---

## 2.5 AI‑Enabled Feedback Loops
AI‑enabled sensing provides continuous insight into:

- Flow  
- Risk  
- Governance  
- Decision latency  
- Cross‑domain patterns  

These insights support adaptive governance and continuous improvement.

# 4. The Enablement Cycle

SC4LE uses a **phased enablement cycle** to establish and mature LDAs:

1. Diagnose  
2. Build  
3. Establish LDA  
4. Embed  
5. Monitor maturity  
6. Withdraw  
7. Move on  

This cycle ensures that LDAs become self‑sustaining and that enablement teams remain mobile.

# 6. How to Use the Operating Model

Use the Operating Model when:

- Establishing or maturing LDAs  
- Designing governance structures  
- Running enablement cycles  
- Supporting teams with decision clarity  
- Diagnosing flow or governance issues  
- Applying shared principles  
- Conducting audits or assurance reviews  
- Scaling SC4LE across domains  

The Operating Model is the **practical guide** for how SC4LE functions day‑to‑day.

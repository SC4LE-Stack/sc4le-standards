# SC4LE Brand Governance  
### Governance of Identity, Visual Language, and Brand Integrity  
© 2026 SC4LE Limited

---

## 1. Purpose of This Document

This document defines how the **SC4LE brand** is governed, protected, and evolved.

It ensures the brand remains:

- consistent  
- recognisable  
- principle‑aligned  
- professionally maintained  
- protected under trademark and licensing  
- coherent across all SC4LE artefacts  

This governance model integrates with:

- `/meta/governance-standard.md`  
- `/meta/governance-content.md`  
- `/meta/governance-diagrams.md`  
- `/meta/governance-messaging.md`  
- `/meta/governance-templates.md`  
- `/meta/website-content-matrix.md`  
- `/meta/website-ia.md`  
- `/meta/website-publishing-playbook.md`  

---

## 2. Scope of Brand Governance

Brand governance applies to:

- SC4LE logo  
- colour palette  
- typography  
- tone of voice  
- naming conventions  
- visual identity  
- iconography  
- diagram styling  
- layout patterns  
- brand assets  
- brand usage rules  

Brand governance **does not** cover:

- operating model content  
- principles  
- diagrams (structural accuracy)  
- templates (structural rules)  

Those are governed separately.

---

## 3. Brand Governance Principles

### **3.1 Identity Integrity**  
The SC4LE brand must remain:

- distinctive  
- modern  
- principle‑aligned  
- consistent across all touchpoints  

### **3.2 Visual Consistency**  
All SC4LE artefacts must use:

- SC4LE colour palette  
- SC4LE typography  
- SC4LE spacing rules  
- SC4LE diagram styling  
- SC4LE iconography  

### **3.3 Messaging Alignment**  
Brand must reinforce:

- SC4LE value propositions  
- SC4LE tone of voice  
- SC4LE naming conventions  
- SC4LE messaging matrix  

### **3.4 No Fragmentation**  
Brand must not be:

- altered  
- forked  
- reinterpreted  
- replaced with derivative identities  

### **3.5 Accessibility**  
Brand must support:

- high contrast  
- readable typography  
- accessible colour combinations  
- screen‑reader compatibility

## 4. Brand Components

### **4.1 Logo**  
Governed by:

- logo usage rules  
- clear space rules  
- minimum size rules  
- colour usage rules  
- background rules  

### **4.2 Colour Palette**  
Defined in:

- `/brand/colour-palette.json`  
- `/design/design-tokens.json`  

Colours must be used consistently across:

- diagrams  
- templates  
- website  
- services  
- programmes  

### **4.3 Typography**  
Defined in:

- `/brand/typography.json`  

Typography must be used consistently across:

- headings  
- body text  
- diagrams  
- templates  
- website  

### **4.4 Tone of Voice**  
Defined in:

- `/brand/tone-of-voice.md`  

Tone must be:

- clear  
- confident  
- principle‑aligned  
- non‑jargon  
- value‑driven  

### **4.5 Naming Conventions**  
Naming must follow:

- SC4LE messaging matrix  
- SC4LE value proposition schema  
- SC4LE service definition schema

## 5. Roles and Responsibilities

### **5.1 SC4LE Brand Editors**  
Responsible for:

- maintaining brand assets  
- ensuring consistency  
- reviewing brand usage  
- preparing updates for approval  

### **5.2 SC4LE Stewardship Board (SSB)**  
Responsible for:

- approving moderate and major brand changes  
- ensuring conceptual integrity  
- protecting brand identity  

### **5.3 SC4LE Limited**  
Holds final authority over:

- brand direction  
- brand licensing  
- brand publication  
- brand enforcement  

---

## 6. Brand Change Classification

### **Minor Change**  
- spacing adjustments  
- accessibility refinements  
- non‑material visual improvements  
**Approval:** Editor

### **Moderate Change**  
- updated colour palette  
- updated typography  
- updated tone of voice  
**Approval:** SSB simple majority

### **Major Change**  
- logo redesign  
- identity redesign  
- naming convention overhaul  
**Approval:** SSB unanimous approval

---

## 7. Brand Workflow

1. **Drafting**  
   Editor drafts brand updates.

2. **Initial Review**  
   Editor classifies change type.

3. **SSB Review**  
   - Minor → bypass  
   - Moderate → simple majority  
   - Major → unanimous approval  

4. **Integration**  
   Editor integrates approved brand updates.

5. **Versioning**  
   Brand follows semantic versioning.

6. **Publication**  
   Brand updates are published using the Website Publishing Playbook.

---

## 8. Versioning Model

Brand follows semantic versioning:

- **MAJOR** — identity redesign  
- **MINOR** — palette/typography/tone updates  
- **PATCH** — corrections or refinements  

---

## 9. Brand Quality Standards

Brand must be:

- clear  
- modern  
- principle‑aligned  
- visually consistent  
- accessible  
- recognisable  
- aligned to SC4LE messaging

## 10. Intellectual Property and Licensing

All SC4LE brand assets are governed by:

- Business Source License 1.1 (BUSL‑1.1)  
- SC4LE trademark policy  
- SC4LE Limited’s commercial rights  

No derivative identities or unlicensed reproductions are permitted.

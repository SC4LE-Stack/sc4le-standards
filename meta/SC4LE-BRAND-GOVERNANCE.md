# SC4LE Brand Governance Model  
### Governance of SC4LE Brand Identity, Visual System, Tone, and Reputation  
© 2026 SC4LE Limited

---

## 1. Purpose of This Document

This document defines how the **SC4LE brand** is governed, maintained, and protected across:

- the SC4LE Standards Repository  
- the SC4LE website  
- all digital and physical brand assets  
- diagrams, templates, and visual frameworks  
- marketing and communication materials  
- AI‑generated content  

It ensures the SC4LE brand remains:

- **strategic, confident, minimal, geometric, premium, and AI‑ready**  
- **consistent across all touchpoints**  
- **aligned to the SC4LE Operating Model and Shared Principles**  
- **protected as intellectual property of SC4LE Limited**

This file governs **brand identity**, not the SC4LE Standard itself.

---

## 2. Relationship to the SC4LE Standard

The SC4LE brand expresses the values, principles, and operating model defined in the SC4LE Standard.

From your uploaded brand source:

> “SC4LE is a modern, AI‑aligned transformation and governance model…  
> The identity must always feel strategic, confident, minimal, geometric, premium, AI‑ready.”

Brand governance ensures that all content, visuals, and messaging remain aligned to:

- SC4LE Shared Principles  
- SC4LE Operating Model  
- SC4LE Governance Model  
- SC4LE Messaging Matrix  
- SC4LE Diagram Governance  

No brand asset may contradict or reinterpret the SC4LE Standard.

---

## 3. Brand Governance Principles

### 3.1 Brand Integrity  
The SC4LE brand must always reflect:

- strategic clarity  
- federated enablement  
- ethical AI  
- modernity and precision  
- minimal, geometric structure  

### 3.2 Golden Source Brand Assets  
The uploaded SC4LE logo and brand system are the **only** approved brand assets.

From your brand pack:

> “Always use the uploaded SC4LE logo as the official and only brand mark.  
> Never recreate, reinterpret, regenerate, or redesign the logo.”

### 3.3 Consistency Across All Touchpoints  
Brand usage must be consistent across:

- website  
- diagrams  
- presentations  
- marketing  
- templates  
- social media  

### 3.4 Clarity Over Complexity  
Visuals must be:

- minimal  
- geometric  
- high‑contrast  
- free of visual noise  

### 3.5 Ethical & Responsible Use of AI  
AI may support brand creation, but must not:

- generate alternative logos  
- invent new brand elements  
- contradict the brand system  
- alter the SC4LE identity  

### 3.6 Reputation Protection  
Brand governance must support:

- trust  
- transparency  
- ethical leadership  
- stakeholder confidence  

From your EPA materials:

> “Brand and reputation are closely linked…  
> Positive reputations drive growth by building confidence and loyalty.”

## 5. Brand Asset Governance

### 5.1 Logo  
The SC4LE logo:

- must only be used in its official form  
- must maintain original proportions  
- must not be recoloured or distorted  
- must not be placed on busy backgrounds  
- must only appear on white, charcoal, or deep‑teal backgrounds  

From your brand pack:

> “Maintain original proportions, colours, gradients, and spacing.”

### 5.2 Colour Palette  
- **Charcoal** — structure  
- **Teal** — active states  
- **Electric Blue** — sensing / AI elements  
- **White** — clarity and contrast  

### 5.3 Typography  
- modern  
- clean  
- high contrast  
- minimal  

### 5.4 Layout  
- generous spacing  
- geometric alignment  
- minimal ornamentation  

---

## 6. Brand Change Types

### 6.1 Minor Change  
- spacing adjustments  
- accessibility improvements  
- export format changes  
**Approval:** Editor

### 6.2 Moderate Change  
- new templates  
- new layout variants  
- new diagram styling (non‑structural)  
**Approval:** Editor + SSB simple majority

### 6.3 Major Change  
- new logo  
- new colour palette  
- new typography  
- new brand system  
- new naming conventions  
**Approval:** SSB unanimous approval  

Brand identity changes are treated as **major** due to IP and reputational impact.

## 7. Brand Workflow

1. **Draft**  
2. **Initial Review** (Editor)  
3. **Classification**  
4. **Approval**  
5. **Integration**  
6. **Versioning**  
7. **Publication**  

This mirrors the SC4LE Standard governance workflow.

---

## 8. Versioning Rules

Brand assets follow semantic versioning:

- **MAJOR** — new brand system  
- **MINOR** — new templates or enhancements  
- **PATCH** — corrections  

Example:  
`v1.2.1`  
- 1 = major brand system  
- 2 = new templates  
- 1 = minor corrections  

---

## 9. Tone of Voice Governance

SC4LE’s tone must always be:

- clear  
- strategic  
- modern  
- confident  
- outcome‑focused  
- principle‑led  

From your brand pack:

> “The identity must always feel strategic, confident, minimal, geometric, premium, AI‑ready.”

Tone must avoid:

- jargon  
- over‑complexity  
- ungrounded claims  
- framework‑led language (SC4LE is principle‑led)

---

## 10. Website & Marketing Brand Governance

All website and marketing content must:

- use the SC4LE Messaging Matrix  
- use canonical value propositions  
- follow the brand system  
- use approved diagrams  
- avoid invented terminology  
- maintain clarity and minimalism  

Website diagrams must follow the **Derived Diagram** rules in `/meta/SC4LE-DIAGRAM-GOVERNANCE.md`.

---

## 11. AI‑Generated Brand Content Rules

AI may support brand content creation, but must not:

- generate new logos  
- reinterpret the SC4LE identity  
- contradict the brand system  
- introduce new brand elements  
- create unapproved colour palettes  

AI outputs must be reviewed by Editors.

---

## 12. Intellectual Property & Licensing

All SC4LE brand assets are protected under:

- **Business Source License 1.1 (BUSL‑1.1)**  
- SC4LE trademark policy  
- SC4LE Limited commercial rights  

No derivative brand assets may be published externally without attribution.

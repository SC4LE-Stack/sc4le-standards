# SC4LE Content Governance Model  
### Governance of SC4LE Content, Messaging, Artefacts & Digital Assets  
© 2026 SC4LE Limited

---

## 1. Purpose of This Document

This document defines how **all SC4LE content** is created, reviewed, approved, versioned, and maintained across:

- the SC4LE Standards Repository  
- the SC4LE website  
- SC4LE diagrams, templates, and messaging  
- brand and marketing materials  
- AI‑generated content  

Its purpose is to ensure that SC4LE content remains:

- **coherent and principle‑aligned**  
- **professionally maintained and traceable**  
- **consistent with the SC4LE Operating Model**  
- **faithful to the golden source**  
- **brand‑safe and IP‑protected**

This file governs **content**, not the SC4LE Standard itself.

---

## 2. Relationship to the SC4LE Standard

The SC4LE Standard is governed separately under **SC4LE-GOVERNANCE.md**, which states:

> “The SC4LE Standards Repository is the authoritative golden source for all SC4LE materials.”  

This Content Governance Model ensures that **all derived content** remains aligned to:

- the SC4LE Operating Model  
- SC4LE Shared Principles  
- SC4LE Governance Model  
- SC4LE Value Propositions  
- SC4LE Brand System  

No content may contradict or extend the standard without formal approval.

---

## 3. Content Governance Principles

### 3.1 Single Golden Source  
All content must trace back to the canonical definitions, diagrams, and terminology stored in the SC4LE Standards Repository.  


### 3.2 Principle‑Led Content  
Content must reflect SC4LE Shared Principles, especially:  
- Clarity Over Complexity  
- Principles Before Processes  
- Flow Before Control  
- Evidence‑Based Decisions  
- Continuous Improvement  


### 3.3 Brand Integrity  
Content must follow the SC4LE brand system:  
- official spiral logo only  
- minimal, geometric visual style  
- strategic, confident tone  


### 3.4 Controlled Evolution  
Content evolves through a structured workflow (Section 6).  
No uncontrolled forks or reinterpretations.

### 3.5 Transparency & Traceability  
All content changes must be versioned, documented, and linked to a change request.

### 3.6 Ethical & Responsible Use of AI  
AI may support content creation, but must not:  
- invent new SC4LE concepts  
- alter the operating model  
- generate alternative logos  
- contradict the golden source  

---

## 4. Scope of Content Governance

This model applies to:

### 4.1 Standards‑Derived Content  
- Operating model descriptions  
- Governance explanations  
- Shared principles  
- Diagrams and frameworks  
- Templates and schemas  

### 4.2 Brand & Marketing Content  
- Website pages  
- Service descriptions  
- Messaging frameworks  
- Presentations  
- Social content  

### 4.3 Visual Assets  
- Diagrams  
- Icons  
- Illustrations  
- Layouts  

### 4.4 AI‑Generated Content  
- Must follow all governance rules  
- Must be reviewed by Editors  
- Must not create new SC4LE intellectual property  

---

## 5. Content Governance Roles

### 5.1 SC4LE Stewardship Board (SSB)  
Ultimate authority for:  
- approving major content changes  
- maintaining conceptual integrity  
- defining the roadmap  


### 5.2 SC4LE Editors  
Responsible for:  
- drafting content  
- ensuring consistency  
- enforcing brand rules  
- preparing release notes  


### 5.3 External Advisors  
May provide input but have **no decision rights**.

### 5.4 Content Contributors  
Anyone producing content for SC4LE:  
- must follow this governance model  
- must not publish without Editor approval  

---

## 6. Content Change Types

### 6.1 Minor Change  
- Typos  
- Formatting  
- Broken links  
- Minor clarity improvements  
**Approval:** Editor

### 6.2 Moderate Change  
- Updated diagrams (non‑structural)  
- Improved explanations  
- New examples  
**Approval:** Editor + SSB simple majority

### 6.3 Major Change  
- New frameworks  
- New diagrams  
- New definitions  
- Changes to principles, roles, or operating model  
**Approval:** Full SSB unanimous approval  


---

## 7. Content Workflow

1. **Draft**  
2. **Initial Review** (Editor)  
3. **Classification** (minor/moderate/major)  
4. **Approval**  
5. **Integration**  
6. **Versioning**  
7. **Publication**  

This mirrors the SC4LE Standard’s governance workflow.  


---

## 8. Versioning Rules

SC4LE uses **semantic versioning**:  
- **MAJOR** — breaking changes  
- **MINOR** — enhancements  
- **PATCH** — corrections  


Example:  
`v1.3.2`  
- 1 = major  
- 3 = minor  
- 2 = patch  

---

## 9. Content Quality Standards

### 9.1 Tone of Voice  
Must follow SC4LE’s canonical tone:  
- clear  
- strategic  
- modern  
- confident  
- outcome‑focused  


### 9.2 Visual Standards  
- minimal  
- geometric  
- high contrast  
- clean spacing  


### 9.3 Accuracy  
Content must:  
- reflect the SC4LE Operating Model  
- use official terminology  
- avoid invention  

### 9.4 Accessibility  
- clear language  
- logical structure  
- alt text for diagrams  
- mobile‑friendly layouts  

---

## 10. Website Content Governance

All website content must:  
- use approved templates  
- follow the SC4LE Messaging Matrix  
- use canonical value propositions  
- follow the SC4LE brand system  

Website content change types:  
- **Minor** → copy edits  
- **Moderate** → new pages  
- **Major** → new sections or new service lines  

---

## 11. AI‑Generated Content Rules

AI may support content creation, but must not:  
- generate new SC4LE intellectual property  
- create or modify the SC4LE logo  
- contradict the golden source  
- introduce new principles, roles, or governance flows  

AI outputs must be reviewed by Editors.

---

## 12. Intellectual Property & Licensing

All SC4LE content is protected under:  
- **Business Source License 1.1 (BUSL‑1.1)**  
- SC4LE trademark policy  
- SC4LE Limited commercial rights  


No governance process may override SC4LE Limited’s ownership.

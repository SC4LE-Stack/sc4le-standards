# SC4LE Services

SC4LE services are the practical mechanisms through which the Strategic Centre for Leading Enablement delivers value across an organisation.  
They translate the SC4LE Operating Model, Shared Principles, and Governance System into **repeatable, high‑impact interventions** that support leadership, teams, and domains.

Each service is designed to be:
- **Lightweight** (low cognitive load)
- **Federated** (supports autonomy)
- **Principle‑led** (aligned to SC4LE Shared Principles)
- **Scalable** (repeatable across domains)
- **Evidence‑based** (supported by sensing and feedback loops)
- **Ethically grounded** (aligned to CDA guardrails)

Services are delivered through a combination of:
- The **Central Design Authority (CDA)**
- **Local Design Authorities (LDAs)**
- **Enablement Teams**
- **Operational Teams**

---

# 1. Purpose of the Services Layer

The Services layer exists to:

- Provide a **clear, structured catalogue** of SC4LE offerings  
- Ensure consistency in how enablement is delivered across domains  
- Support the **Enablement Cycle** (Diagnose → Build → Establish → Embed → Monitor → Withdraw)  
- Enable predictable, high‑quality outcomes  
- Reduce duplication and fragmentation  
- Provide a shared language for transformation and governance work  
- Anchor SC4LE’s value proposition for internal and external stakeholders  

Services are the **delivery engine** of SC4LE.

---

# 2. How SC4LE Services Fit Into the Operating Model

SC4LE services sit on top of the Foundations and Operating Model layers:

- **Shared Principles** guide how services are delivered  
- **CDA** provides coherence, standards, and cross‑domain governance  
- **LDAs** provide contextual interpretation and local decision‑making  
- **Operational Teams** consume services and generate sensing signals  
- **AI‑enabled feedback loops** inform continuous improvement  

Services are the **practical expression** of the Operating Model.

---

# 3. Structure of a SC4LE Service

Every SC4LE service follows the same canonical structure:

- **Purpose**  
- **Problem it solves**  
- **Who it serves**  
- **Inputs**  
- **Outputs**  
- **Activities**  
- **Success measures**  
- **Governance interactions** (Team → LDA → CDA)  
- **Sensing signals**  
- **Risks & mitigations**  
- **Versioning & ownership**

This structure ensures clarity, consistency, and auditability.

# SC4LE Governance Model  
### Governance of the SC4LE Standard  
© 2026 SC4LE Limited

---

## 1. Purpose of This Document

This document defines how the SC4LE Standard is governed, maintained, and evolved.  
It ensures that SC4LE remains:

- coherent  
- professionally maintained  
- versioned and traceable  
- aligned to its core principles  
- protected as the golden source  

SC4LE is a governed standard, not a community‑driven open‑source project.  
SC4LE Limited retains full authority over its direction, content, and evolution.

---

## 2. Governance Principles

1. **Single Source of Truth**  
   The SC4LE Standards Repository is the authoritative golden source for all SC4LE materials.

2. **Controlled Evolution**  
   Changes to the standard follow a structured review and approval process.

3. **Principle‑Led Decision Making**  
   All changes must align with SC4LE’s core principles, operating model, and purpose.

4. **Transparency**  
   The evolution of the standard is visible through versioning, release notes, and documented decisions.

5. **Integrity of the Framework**  
   SC4LE must remain internally consistent, professionally credible, and free from fragmentation.

---

## 3. Governance Roles

### 3.1 SC4LE Stewardship Board (SSB)  
The SSB is the governing authority for the SC4LE Standard.  
It is responsible for:

- approving or rejecting changes  
- maintaining conceptual integrity  
- ensuring alignment with SC4LE principles  
- defining the roadmap for the standard  
- managing version releases  

The SSB is operated by **SC4LE Limited**.

### 3.2 SC4LE Editors  
Editors are appointed by SC4LE Limited and are responsible for:

- drafting updates  
- maintaining documentation quality  
- ensuring consistency across artefacts  
- preparing release notes  

Editors do not have approval authority.

### 3.3 External Advisors (Optional)  
Industry experts may be invited to provide input.  
They have **no decision rights**.

---

## 4. Change Management Process

All changes to the SC4LE Standard follow a structured workflow.

### 4.1 Change Types

- **Minor Change**  
  Typos, formatting, clarifications, non‑material updates.  
  Approved by Editors.

- **Moderate Change**  
  Adjustments to diagrams, definitions, or descriptions that do not alter the operating model.  
  Requires SSB review.

- **Major Change**  
  Any change that affects:  
  - principles  
  - roles  
  - governance flows  
  - operating model structure  
  - core definitions  
  - SC4LE diagrams  
  Requires full SSB approval.

---

## 5. Change Workflow

1. **Proposal**  
   A change request is drafted by an Editor or SC4LE Limited.

2. **Initial Review**  
   Editors assess the change type and prepare a recommendation.

3. **SSB Review**  
   - Minor: bypass  
   - Moderate: review + simple majority  
   - Major: full review + unanimous approval

4. **Integration**  
   Editors integrate approved changes into the golden source.

5. **Versioning**  
   Each release is tagged using semantic versioning (see Section 6).

6. **Publication**  
   Release notes are published in the repository.

---

## 6. Versioning Model

SC4LE uses **semantic versioning**:

- **MAJOR** — breaking changes to the operating model  
- **MINOR** — enhancements, new definitions, updated diagrams  
- **PATCH** — corrections, clarifications, formatting  

Example:  
`v1.3.2`  
- 1 = major version  
- 3 = minor enhancements  
- 2 = patch fixes

## 7. Golden Source Rules

The SC4LE Standards Repository is the **only** authoritative source of:

- the SC4LE Operating Model  
- SC4LE diagrams  
- SC4LE principles  
- SC4LE governance  
- SC4LE terminology  
- SC4LE schemas and definitions  

No external forks, mirrors, or derivative frameworks are permitted.

Partners and practitioners must reference the golden source directly.

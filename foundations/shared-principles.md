# SC4LE Shared Principles

Shared Principles are the foundation of the SC4LE operating model.  
They reduce cognitive load, accelerate decision‑making, and create alignment without heavy governance.  
These principles guide behaviour across teams, functions, and leadership levels, enabling federated autonomy with strategic coherence.

The principles must be applied consistently across all SC4LE services, governance structures, and operating model components.

---

## 1. Principle: **Clarity Over Complexity**

Decisions, governance, and communication must prioritise clarity.  
Ambiguity increases cognitive load, slows flow, and creates unnecessary escalation.

**Behaviours**
- Use simple, direct language.  
- Make decision pathways explicit.  
- Remove unnecessary steps, artefacts, and approvals.  
- Prioritise transparency over completeness.

---

## 2. Principle: **Principles Before Processes**

Processes support alignment, but principles drive behaviour.  
Teams should use principles to make context‑appropriate decisions without waiting for permission.

**Behaviours**
- Apply principles when processes are unclear or insufficient.  
- Use principles to resolve conflicts and trade‑offs.  
- Avoid rigid, process‑first thinking.  
- Adapt processes when principles indicate a better path.

---

## 3. Principle: **Federated Autonomy**

Teams closest to the work make decisions, supported by Local Design Authorities (LDAs) and shared principles.  
Autonomy is granted with alignment, not in isolation.

**Behaviours**
- Push decisions to the lowest responsible level.  
- Use LDAs to maintain coherence across domains.  
- Escalate only when principles cannot resolve the issue.  
- Balance local optimisation with organisational alignment.

---

## 4. Principle: **Sensing Before Steering**

Decisions should be informed by real‑time sensing, feedback loops, and early indicators — not assumptions or lagging metrics.

**Behaviours**
- Use data to detect risk early.  
- Integrate sensing into governance and flow.  
- Prioritise leading indicators over lagging ones.  
- Adjust decisions based on new signals.

---

## 5. Principle: **Flow as a First‑Class Outcome**

Flow is a strategic asset.  
Reducing friction, rework, and delay improves value delivery, reduces cost, and strengthens organisational resilience.

**Behaviours**
- Identify and remove systemic bottlenecks.  
- Minimise handoffs and unnecessary approvals.  
- Reduce rework through clarity and alignment.  
- Treat flow issues as governance issues.

---

## 6. Principle: **Build, Embed, Withdraw**

Enablement is temporary.  
The goal is to uplift capability, embed governance, and withdraw once teams become self‑sustaining.

**Behaviours**
- Focus on capability, not dependency.  
- Transfer ownership early and progressively.  
- Build LDAs to sustain governance locally.  
- Withdraw once maturity criteria are met.

---

## 7. Principle: **Continuous Improvement as Culture**

Improvement is not an event — it is a continuous, embedded behaviour supported by sensing, reflection, and learning.

**Behaviours**
- Run regular improvement cycles.  
- Use sensing to identify improvement opportunities.  
- Encourage experimentation and learning.  
- Treat improvement as part of daily work, not a project.

---

## Summary

SC4LE Shared Principles provide the alignment mechanism that enables federated governance, accelerates decision‑making, and reduces cognitive load.  
They are the backbone of the SC4LE operating model and must be applied consistently across all teams, services, and governance structures.
